The Film
Borat (2006)

A mockumentary following fictional Kazakh journalist Borat Sagdiyev as he travels across the USA. Directed by Larry Charles, starring Sacha Baron Cohen. Won a Golden Globe. Controversial, beloved, and deeply satirical.

The Character
Borat Sagdiyev

A fictional journalist performed by British comedian Sacha Baron Cohen. Real people are interviewed without knowing they are part of a satirical film — their unscripted reactions reveal real social attitudes.

Why does it matter?

Borat uses the device of a naïve outsider to expose prejudices that Americans might never voice openly. When people believe they are talking to a genuine — if strange — foreign journalist, they let down their guard. The irony is that Borat's own exaggerated offensive views become a mirror: audiences laugh at Borat, but the real joke is on the people who agree with him.
